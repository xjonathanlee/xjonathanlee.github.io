Musical DNA

Samurai Champloo expanded my knowledge in music and introduced me to Seba Jun (Nujabes) at age 13. When he passed in 2008 I was intimately familiar with his music that fused both jazz and hiphop. This project was a response to his albums he produced both solo and with his collective Hydeout Productions.

  • Roles:Art Direction, Research Creation
  • Tools: Adobe Illustrator, Interaction Design, Data Synthesis
  • Context: Personal Project
  • Deliverables: Poster, Dataset

Problem

Excersing our skills in data design how might we abstract Seba Jun's discography?

Project Brief

From the start I knew that I wanted to create a long form poster that would feature a way to catalogue DNA from across Nujabes entire discography, something like how you would read a DNA CRSPR slide only more visual. This process of creating a reasonable yet easy to read visual took a long, but fun process of iteration.

Notation

During the research and synthesis phase I found myself looking through ways to express "lineage" of music specifically how to illustrate interconnectedness within samples and tracks. Using CRSPR notion and documenting patterns seemed like an obvious choice as the symbolic references to DNA and "Musical DNA" seemed clear.

After a conversation with a music theory teacher I was introduced to the ideas of avante garde music notion from the 1950's. Introducing me to the notions of abstracting shapes into sounds.

A visual barometer of functional and expressive musical scores.

Iteration 1

Iteration 1 took the form of an abstracted beat map. I wanted to look at how we could position the samples of each track chronologically, leading to a more cohesive but visually messy infographic. In the spirit of designing this experiement,I was "honest" in its execution, as it showed the difficulty of transcribing and visualizing wide swathes of information and presenting them for reading.

Visually Cool (and very Data Human), but a lot of work for each song.

Product

I wanted to see if I could push functionality to allow for a better user experience digitally. Physically the poster was very long, hampering some readability. I chose to add functionality by adding a holistic zoom feature digitally that allowed users to view things more closely.
