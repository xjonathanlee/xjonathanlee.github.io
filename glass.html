Glass - Documentary

A video shot for IAT 336 to explain and illustrate the properties and uses of glass. Leveraging Lucas' mastery of the material, we wanted to illustrate the potential use cases for glass and how glass' unique properties can be leveraged

  • Roles: Art Direction, Visual Design, Videography, Interview Prep
  • Tools: Adobe Audition, Adobe Premier, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe After Effects
  • Context: Mentorship Project
  • Team: Henry Lin, Jonathan Lee

problem

How do we create accessible moments of learning for students during the pandemic?

design process

In Fall of 2020, a mentor expressed interest in helping me develop a capstone video project for the IAT336 course -- Materials in Design. The goal of the video was to explore materials and design methodologies outside of the SIAT norm. In this case, we chose to focus on SFU creatives within the science faculty that used materials that many students donâ€™t have access to.
Over the course of a 15 hour marathon shoot, we decided to write, edit and document the process of turning a raw material into a minimum viable product, all while exploring the properties and materiality of glass within a professional practice. (NB: Subject to change as covid restrictions prevents us from acquiring additional audio or footage)

Precedence

Our explorations lead us to first study the methodology of creating a short film documentary contextually within the realm of glass, while respecting the rules and regulations set by the school. Our process was drawn from previous documentary teams from SIAT that focused on nordic and italian design culture.
