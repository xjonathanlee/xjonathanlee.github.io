Off*Line YVR

INFO

Offline (Stylized as Off*Line) is an anime events collective. Specializing in Anikura events, they hold monthly "Anime Raves" with touches of Japanese Culture. I specifically researched and designed different ways to interact with the crowd via webcam and reactive motion graphics, I also looked at optimization of crowds and passive data collection.

CONTEXT

Under my tenure and with assistance from the crew we looked at different ways to interact with and involve the audience as well as improve future experiences through writing documents and training staff to better perform jobs like Video Jockey-ing and optimizations to in event activations. Leveraging Snapchat / and tactile mediums I used UX principles to alieve long lines and optimize events.

Problem

*How might we design a way to enhance the audience experience in a chaotic space?

Training Manual

Ron, the Head VJ, and I noticed a major challenge when training newer VJs with little to no production experience; the process was informal, scattered, and often required people to learn during larger live production events. One of the biggest obstacles was the lack of available resources for staff to train others effectively, along with limited momentum to keep training initiatives moving forward. To allow our trainees to flourish we've created a basic training program to get trainees to grasp the basics in less than 60 minutes. By using, remixing and indexing exsisting VJ loops we were able to get trainees to debut at live shows after 1 month of training.

Shaders + Phasers

Reimagining how VJing could enhance the crowd experience became one of our primary goals while developing this new technical system. To support this vision, we introduced new phasers and shaders that allowed for greater creative control and more detailed visual expression throughout our performances. We leveraged our backgrounds in UX design and literal cooking to orchestrate "highs and lows" similar to set meals and more conventional DJ sets at festivals.

UX optimizations and Lineups

An unintended affordance of hosting and running Anikura events means that as a UX and Social Events organizer we could use lines and pre-events as "living labs" in order to test new theories and ideas, or in this case, to confirm passive data collection through events was a viable operations strategy. This allowed OFFLINE to gather more data from event-goers to optimize, and plan future events. Inspired by Giorgia Lupi's and Laurie Frick's work at TED, MOMA, and PENTAGRAM I wanted to use the ideas of data art as a way to enhance events through passive data collection turned into art. Our affordance here was that users would be able to interact with a pre experience before the event and helping to alieviate bordom through an engaged tactile activity also give users an impetus to talk and foster new connections through unmediated interactions.

As part of a series of living lab experiments I looked at precedence to engage and spark discussion between new groups of people:

Data Wall

At its core, a data wall is a low-tech tool that does two things well: it reframes how participants think about a question, and it generates real data for future event planning. By encoding responses through strings and nails, stickers and pegs, or a mix of both, we give attendees a way to express what's on their minds.

The format also has practical benefits. Moving the activity to a wall frees up floor space and keeps traffic flowing through the event space. And perhaps best of all, a well-executed data wall becomes participatory art, and a genuine point of connection for organizers at the end of the day. My initial test of the data wall functioned as a prototype to test the feasibility of the idea in a "Anime -Club" Adjacent setting, participants answering questions

Idea Expansion

Granularity can be important in data art as it allows users to connect with each other at a more-than-surface level. While we confirmed the concept is sound, I wanted to explore more specific questions related to the event. Using this as a catalyst I prepped the next event to re-use the same concept but to ask more provoking questions to better gauge and gather data, while also alluding to and teasing future events.
