Off*Line YVR

INFO

Offline (Stylized as Off*Line) is an anime DJ Collective. Specializing in Anikai events, they hold monthly "Anime Raves" with infusions of Japanese Culture. I specifically researched and designed different ways to interact with the crowd via webcam and reactive motion graphics.

CONTEXT

Under my tenure and with assistance from the crew we looked at different ways to interact with and involve the audience as well as improve future experiences through writing documents and training staff to better perform jobs like Video Jockey-ing and optimizations to in event activities.

Problem

How might we design a way to enhance the audience experience in a chaotic dance fueled space?

Training Manual

Ron, the Head VJ, and I noticed a major challenge when training newer VJs with little to no production experience; the process was informal, scattered, and often required people to learn during larger live production events. One of the biggest obstacles was the lack of available resources for staff to train others effectively, along with limited momentum to keep training initiatives moving forward.

During one of our mid-sized events, I realized we needed a more structured and approachable way to keep training on track while also promoting inclusivity within the group. I proposed creating a VJ training manual to help new team members become comfortable in a fast-paced role where they needed to stay synchronized with both the DJ and the setlist. The manual focused on building crystallized knowledge through MIDI controller drills and tapping exercises designed to improve familiarity and confidence; trainees would then apply these fundamentals in a pre-recorded set environment to test and reinforce their skills.

In addition, Ron and I explored new ways to improve the guest experience by overhauling visual shaders in Resolume. As we developed the training manual, we also maintained a detailed records log documenting how to recreate specific effects and configure supplemental production elements. This included workflows for VJ-to-DJ interfacing through custom Discord-linked scripts, as well as documentation of our experiments with Python development for shows that incorporated Xbox Kinect integration.

Shaders + Phasers

Reimagining how VJing could enhance the crowd experience became one of our primary goals while developing this new technical system. To support this vision, we introduced new phasers and shaders that allowed for greater creative control and more detailed visual expression throughout our performances.

By incorporating hue churn and glitch phaser effects into our DJ programming through custom shaders and animations, we were able to create visuals that felt more dynamic and immersive for the audience. We also used After Effects to source, edit, and organize media that reflected the diversity of our visual styles; by compiling these assets into a centralized master file, we created a flexible library of visual “moods” that could be quickly adapted to match different performances and atmospheres.
